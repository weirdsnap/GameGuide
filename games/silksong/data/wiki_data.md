The <b>Citadel</b> is a late-game area in Pharloom. It is located at the very top of the kingdom. Hornet must ascend to the Citadel on her quest through Pharloom. The Citadel has been often described as "shining" and "holy".

# 文档：The Mists

- 类别：areas
- 标识：the mists
- 来源：wiki/The_Mists

The Mist is a secret area accessed through Bilewater and a breakable wall in Sinners road. A treacherous illusion conjured by Phantom, the Mist serves to hide the Exhaust Organ used to expel the choking refuse of the Citadel.

Description
The entrance of the Mist is a pathway with several dead moorbugs like thread rakers, roach feeders and roach catchers scattered about

Apart from small groups of Silkflies the only lifeforms, if one could call them that, populating the area are Wraiths that hover motionlessly in the air until Hornet comes too close, at which point they will scream and lunge at her, draining her of life and silk upon making contact.

Blind movement will get her nowhere, but not all rooms are dangerous. Some smaller ones contain only Silkflies. If the Needolin is played near the Silkflies, they will guide Hornet towards the correct exit until she finally reaches the Exhaust Organ. If Hornet goes through the wrong exit or Falls into the mist-covered pits it will put Hornet back at the entrance to the mist.

Defeating Phantom will give you the Cross Stitch spell and the Mist will become inaccessible putting you straight infront of the exhaust organ. The exhaust organ will remain as a sub-area of Bilewater.

How to Access
The Mist is located at the bottom of  Bilewater, on the opposite side of the area's Bellway station. They additionally can be reached by breaking a wall on the left side of the left room after ascending the main room connected to the first bench in sinner’s road, connecting to the aforementioned entrance.

# 文档：Bell Beast

- 类别：bosses
- 标识：bell beast
- 来源：wiki/Bell_Beast

SS Infobox NPC
|dlc = silksong
|image1 = Enemy 9.png
|gender = Female
|area = The Marrow The Bell Beast appears to be the main method of fast travel throughout Pharloom. She is initially found in the Marrow, where she is trapped in Silk which can only be cleared by a Silkspear attack. Once the Bell Beast is freed, she attacks Hornet. After her defeat, she can be used to travel between the different bell stations. In Act 3 she has babies.

Behavior and Tactics
The Bell Beast has three main attacks:

Charge: Bell Beast digs in the ground for a moment, without making any growling noises, before surfacing and rushing at Hornet.

Launch: Bell Beast digs in the ground, similar to the charge attack, but growls before jumping  up in the air and arching to land on the other side of the arena.

Throw: Bell Beast silently digs in the ground, with a smaller width than with the other attacks, before launching a bell on both sides of herself. Each bell, when thrown with this attack, bounces once before rolling to either edge of the arena and rolls off the edges of the screen.

Bell Beast will always start the battle with a Charge attack starting from the right side of the arena.

After each attack, Bell Beast returns to momentarily hide underground. Once you're in the second battle phase, any attack has a chance to make one bell fall from the ceiling, bouncing once before passing through the ground.

To free her, you need the Silk Spear skill.

Unlike The Last Stag, she does not appear to have any dialogue.

Bell Beast does double damage with all attacks, including falling bells. She enters Phase 2 at 15 hits (Silk Spear does 3 hits) after a scream. In this phase, Bell Beast will cause a bell to fall from the roof after every attack, telegraphed by dust falling from the ceiling. These bells bounce once and then crumble. The fight ends at around 30 hits. PS: (one "hit" is 5 damage)

Trivia
The Bell Beast is visually very similar to the Dirtcarver.
While there is no option to interact with the Bell Beast, hitting her with your needle allows you to "pet" the Bell Beast. She enjoys this by purring and playing with the ground.
Using the Needolin ability near the Bell Beast, she starts to purr. However, there is no special dialogue upon doing so.
After defeating the Bell Eater in Act 3, it's revealed that the Bell Beast has children, and can allow Hornet to access the Bell Beast via the Beastling Call ability w/ the Needolin.
You can juggle the Beastlings with your needle. Very fun activity to do if you are bored.

# 文档：Cogwork Dancers

- 类别：bosses
- 标识：cogwork dancers
- 来源：wiki/Cogwork_Dancers

The Cogwork Dancers are a duo boss in Hollow Knight: Silksong. These bosses are found in the Cogwork Core of The Citadel. The Cogwork Dancers move in sync with each other, with white lines choreographing their movement. This boss has 4 phases.

Behavior
Phase 1: Both dancers will move at the same time in the direction of their white line. Occasionally they will create a circle in the middle which creates a barrage of slashes. They can also create a bunch of lines across the arena and cut across them rapidly.

Phase 2: Both dancers are much faster.

Phase 3: The dancers now move one at a time, out of sync.

Phase 4: After 1 dancer dies, the other one will become extremely slow. They will attempt to do their circle barrage attack, but without their partner, they simply float slowly to the ground.

Note: Prior to the start of each phase, the cogs in the background will wind up the dancers accordingly. Before the first phase, the cogs wind up normally. Before the second, they wind up noticeably faster. Before the third, they take turns winding up one at a time. Before the final phase, the remaining dancer’s cogs wind up slowly.

# 文档：Fourth Chorus

- 类别：bosses
- 标识：fourth chorus
- 来源：wiki/Fourth_Chorus

Fourth Chorus is a boss in Hollow Knight: Silksong. It appears in Deep Docks after completing the Flexile Spines Quest in Far Fields and receiving the Drifters Cloak cloak upgrade from The Seamstress. It's described as a metal automation that works in the Deep Docks.

In the first phase of the fight, it has two main attacks: it slams down vertically with one of its two top hands, or swipes horizontally across the screen with one of its bottom two hands. The vertical attack temporarily destroys the platform underneath it, so Hornet cannot stand on it. Falling into the lava there deals two masks of damage. The boss only takes damage from hits on its head, since the rest of its body is a sturdy metal.

Once Fourth Chorus has staggered, another attack is added, which causes lava balls to rain down from the ceiling. Coming into contact with one does one mask of damage.

In the final stage of the fight, which comes after 3 staggers, upward drafts will appear to the left and right of the battle platform, which is now slightly smaller. While it's possible to beat Fourth Chorus by attacking its head until it falls, it can also be defeated indirectly. This can be done when the upward drafts show up, by riding them to the top and hitting the magma blocks (one on each side) in the ceiling. This causes a Boulder to fall and crush the boss's head.

Grand Mother Silk is one of the main bosses in Hollow Knight: Silksong. She is the main antagonist of Hollow Knight: Silksong.

Lore
At first, Weavers were free from her for the first time, and some had even left Pharloom for other kingdoms, such as Hallownest. However, the Bugs of Pharloom, specifically those in the Whiteward, used the mother's Silk in order to increase their lifespan, which led to the Grand Mother being able to control these bugs at her will. She had hated what the Weavers had done to her, so she commanded the Bugs of Pharloom to venture out of their kingdom in order to find the Weavers, which had included Hornet despite also being part Wyrm.

Weavers had also made Weavenests across Pharloom . They were outposts in which Weavers tested out experiments. The most notable Weavenest is Weavenest Atla, where Hornet encounters Eva. A being made purely of Silk, in an attempt to recreate a Higher Being who could challenge the Grand Mother Silk. However, Eva's form was too frail to exist outside her chamber.

In-game events
Behaviour and tactics
Grand Mother Silk functions similarly to the Radiance, in that she does not deal contact damage and is also attacked at the head. Instead of teleporting like the Radiance, she swiftly moves around and launches sets of nails either vertically down or horizontally in the direction Hornet is in. She also has one of the few moves in the game that deals 3 masks of damage.

Location
Grand Mother Silk is located at the top of The Cradle.

# 文档：Gurr the Outcast

- 类别：bosses
- 标识：gurr the outcast
- 来源：wiki/Gurr_the_Outcast

<b>Gurr the Outcast</b> is an optional boss  in Hollow Knight: Silksong. He's located in a secret room in Far Fields

In-game events
After starting act 3, a new wish will appear on Bellhart's wish wall : "The hidden hunter", which requests you to track down Gurr. New traps are added in the way to him and the last tracking object is in reality a cage which will bring you down to his arena. The fight will start shortly after.
Behaviour and Tactics

<b>Trap Throw</b>: Gurr leaps into the air, either vertically or diagonally, and throws 2 Barbed Traps in succession that float midair in random locations throughout the arena. After a few moments, traps extend their spikes into a large circle, then disappear. If Hornet hits a trap, it expands immediately. After reaching 750 HP, Gurr always throws out 3 traps instead. When this attack is finished, Gurr can either land back on the floor or immediately dive beneath the ground.
<b>Slide</b>:  Gurr hunches down and slides across the arena, only stopping when he reaches a wall. Upon reaching a wall, he can either perform another attack or immediately burrow down.
<b>Floor Spikes</b>:  When Gurr is above ground, he makes a small jump up and dive downwards into the ground. The ground then rumbles as 3 sets of spikes shoot up in random positions across the ground, then retract after a short time. The positions of the spikes are telegraphed by small puffs of dust.
<b>Trap Flurry</b>:  Once per fight at 400 HP, Gurr performs this attack; signalled by him shrieking while underground. After a moment, Gurr leaps out of the ground in the very centre of the arena and throws 5 Barbed Traps in succession around the room.
<b>Leap</b>: Gurr makes large leaps around the arena to reposition for attacks.
<b>Backroll</b>: If Hornet is too close to Gurr after an attack, he quickly rolls away a set distance.

Needolin dialogue
Gurr the Outcast does not respond to the Needolin during battle. The following dialogue can be found in the room to the left of the arena :
<b>...The last...</b>
<b>...The hunt...
<b>...Pale...prey...
<b>...Come to me...

Location
Gurr can be found in Far Fields after promising the wish "The hidden hunter".

Moorwing is an enemy and boss in Hollow Knight: Silksong, and can be found in the Greymoor area left of the Bellway station.

The Moorwing has 3 different attacks in phase 1, those being a side dive, many quick slashes downwards and throwing a bone saw along the ground. In phase 2 it gains a few new moves including  throwing two bone saws in a circle downwards making you have to jump twice and the phase 1 bone saw move, but with two saws instead of just one. The Moorwing will also fall, so make sure you're not beneath it, or you will be dealt 2 masks of damage.

Appearance
Moorwing has brownish wings and two teeth, and kind of looks like a big fly.

Strategy
During the first phase, you can stay under it and attack upwards to send it up. The Moorwing attacks will start slightly in a horizontal direction, allowing you to dash out of the way. If you have the drifter's cloak, you can float above the saws instead of jumping, making it much easier to avoid the double saws in phase 2. Remaining below the Moorwing while the circular saws are being thrown will also allow you to only jump once to avoid them. The best time to heal is generally while the Moorwing is making a multi-slash, or immediately after you avoid the side dive. Alternatively, you can pogo on top of the boss, greatly negating the difficulty of dodging all of its attacks

How she is located in the world is explained to be similar to how Cornifer could be found in <i>Hollow Knight</i>; scattered golden rings signal her presence instead of scattered pages.  If Shakra can't be found, she can be called by striking the ring on the platform to the right and above the bell station in Bellhart. Shakra refers to this area as her camp, where she returns to after she's done exploring and looking for her master.

Shakra is shown as a fighter in universe, she fights alongside hornet after the first wave in the ant arena in Hunter's march, if she's still in the area. She bounces her ring around to attack from a range. After the Trail's End wish is completed and Moorwing has been killed, Shakra can be dueled where Moorwing was fought. # 文档：Silk

- 类别：combat
- 标识：silk
- 来源：wiki/Silk

<b>Silk</b> is a unique force that acts as the life-force for bugs in Pharloom. Similar to its counterpart, Soul, it can be manipulated into spells and healing, but it can also be used to power up machines and automatons.

In-game, Silk is represented on the HUD by a spool with several strands. Hornet starts with a broken spool, only able to hold 9 strands of Silk. When the maximum amount of strands are reached, they glow a white light. Silk accumulated in the extended Spool is smaller and more dim, but function the exact same. When Hornet dies, her Spool becomes broken and can only hold 9 Strands of Silk.

Lore
Silk is a beneficial force that can extend life and heal wounds that is found in almost every bug in Pharloom. Strands of Silk can be seen in places such as the Mossy Grotto, and when unlocking Silk hearts, they form around in the air before combining into the Heart. Bugs of Pharloom, especially those in the Whiteward, stored Silk in spindles, and have also recorded certain surgeries for injecting Silk into bugs.

Silk can be used to channel Silk Skills, such as Silkspear, Thread Storm, Sharpdart and Rune Rage which were made by the Weavers. Other skills such as Cross Stitch come from a special technique from Phantom, and Pale Nails which is a move created by the Grand Mother Silk herself. Many tools interact with Silk as well. Injector Bands increase the speed of binding and Spool Extender allows one to hold more Silk.

The bugs in Whiteward used Silk as a means to extend the life of their surrounding bugs, but generations of surgery lead to the Grand Mother to gain control over them, even while asleep. Many bugs and clans attempted to resist the Haunting, with notable examples being the Green Princes of Verdania and the Skarr Clan. However, they have weakened and most of their inhabitants either died or fell victim to the Haunting.

Hornet uses her Needle to gain Silk by striking enemies, as well as using spindles across the Citadel and Webs found in places such as Mount Fay.Usage
Silk can be used to cast Silk Skills or to Bind, which heals Hornet 3 masks in a single instance. When there enough Silk to cast a Silk Skill, if equipped, the icon of the Silk Skill glows white. If there is enough Silk to Bind, then the Silk Spool's threads glow White accompanied by a soft sound effect.

Binding requires 9 Silk and only heals 3 masks. This can be increased to 4 when using the Multibinder tool. Casting Silk Skills cost 4 Silk each, but can be reduced to 3 using the Egg of Flealia tool.

Some Ancestral Arts also require Silk. Each Art requires 1 Silk each, with the exception of Needolin since it uses Silk over time. Pressing Battle Stance also expends Silk unless you have the Throwing Rings equipped.

The Silkshot tool is the only tool that requires Silk to be used, using 1 Silk per attack.

Acquiring Silk
Attacking Enemies
Enemies struck with the Needle cause Hornet to gain Silk at a rate of 1 strand per hit.

Silk Spindles
Silk Spindles are found across all of Pharloom. When hit with the Needle, they release Strands of Silk that are absorbed, granting 3 Strand of Silk each time they are struck. After a certain number of hits, the Silk Spindle runs out and no longer gives Silk. They are similar to <i>Hollow Knight's</i> Soul Totems.

Webs
Webs are found all across Pharloom. There are webs on sets of vines, potentially left by Weavers. Each web gives 3 silk strands.

Tools
Certain tools may help with the acquiring of Silk, like Druid's Eye and Weavelight. Some tools increase the Spool length like Spool Extender.

Sylphsong
Essence of a being merged within. Silk will be restored when resting at a bench.

Requires using 18 Memory Lockets on any crest besides the Hunter's crest and obtaining all previous rewards from Eva. Works exactly the same as Salubra's Blessing in Hollow Knight.

Hot Springs
Only 2 Hot Springs exist in Silksong: one in the Citadel Spa and the other in the Flea Caravan. Generates Silk at the same rate as Sylphsong.

Silk Spool
Silk Spools are additional storage for Silk, represented as an extension to the original Spool. Hornet can collect 9 Silk Spools throughout the game by collecting Spool Fragments. 2 Spool Fragments are required to make one Silk Spool. Each Spool holds 1 strand of Silk.

If Hornet gains silk if the original Spool is full, it is instantly transferred to the next empty spool, starting with the closest leftmost one.

Unlike Hollow Knight, Silk does not need to be transferred from Spools to the Main Spool, and there is no delay for transferring Silk between Spools. Also, Hornet does not gain Silk at a reduced rate for the extended Spools.
Trivia
Silkspear, Thread Storm and Sharpdart are all moves used by Hornet in her boss fight in Hollow Knight. However, Silkspear and Sharpdart are quicker with different animations from the boss fight. Thread Storm remains the exact same, with the exception of spamming the attack move increases the duration.
Battle Stance has the exact same animation as the one used in the boss fight.
Beast's Crest changes the animation of Battle Stance to a more upright stance with her needle facing down and opposite of where Hornet is facing. Hornet's voice also becomes more raspy.
Using Throwing Rings with Battle Stance does not expend silk and is identical to Shakra's idle after walking away from her.

Zango was Zylotol's assistant, helping him with his Plasmium researches. At one point, he planted 3 Plasmium seeds and died in a cavern, seemingly because of Plasmium.

In-game events
When going into the Wormays, the player can enter a cavern and stumble across Zango's body, covered in Plasmium, dead. After starting act 3, the Wormways become infested with Plasmium, with modified enemies. By going into the same cavern, Plasmified Zango will start the fight.

Behaviour and Tactics
<b>Heal</b>: Plasmified Zango will constantly regenrate health if he's not attacked.
<b>Walk</b>: Zango will walk forward like a regular enemy, turning around only when he reaches the side of the arena.
<b>Plasmium Blobs</b>: The boss will violently shake, causing Plasmium blobs to be ejected in all of the arena. The less health he has, the more blobs spawns.
<b>Charge</b>: When reaching half of his life, Zango will replace his walk with a charge.

Needolin dialogue
Zango will not respond to the Needolin during it's fight . But using the Needolin by his corpse before Act 3 will trigger a Needolin memory :
<b>To our dead lands... life... </b>
<b>The buds must grow...
<b>Only a little... Only a spark...
<b>Too dark...

Location
Zango's corpse can be found in a cave at the bottom left of the Wormays. He can be fought in the same place after starting act 3.

<b>Tormented Trobbio</b> is an optional boss  in Hollow Knight: Silksong. He's located in The Stage in the Whispering Vaults.

Behaviour and Tactics

Phase 1
Fly: Tormented Trobbio uses his wings to fly across the arena.
Floorboard Shuffle: Tormented  Trobbio descends into the floorboards to traverse to the opposite end of the arena.
<b>Woeful Whirlwind</b>: Tormented Trobbio spins, creating a tornado that goes horizontally while leaving trails of explosion in its path. If the attack is used while Trobbio is in midair, the tornado slowly descends while still leacing the explosive trail.

<b>Smoke Bombs</b>: Tormented Trobbio throws 3 smoke bombs that explodes either in a plus or cross shape. He can throw them either while being underground or on the arena.Phase 2

<b>Pyrotechnics</b>: Trobbio disappears underground, before 5 fire pillars shoot out from the ground from random locations and at staggered intervals. The pillars are telegraphed by a fizzle coming from under the floor where they appear. Trobbio is launched into the air from within the fifth fire pillar, then 2 more pillars shoots out.

<b>Rosaries</b> are the currency of Pharloom used in <i>Hollow Knight: Silksong</i>.  Rosaries are visually represented by 3 variations that differ in value when dropped by enemies while in the player HUD and Inventory they are visually represented by the default red beads next to their count

Variations
There are 3 known variations of rosaries that differ in value.

Rosaries: The first variation of rosaries each is worth 1 bead
Shell rosaries: The second variation of rosaries each is worth 5 beads
Pearl rosaries: The final and highest valued variation of rosaries each is worth 15 beads

Rosary Strings
Rosaries can be strung together forming a Rosary String/Necklace, that store a number of rosaries and prevents them from loss upon death, Strung rosaries can't be spent until the Rosary string/necklace is broken

<b>Shell Shards</b> are a collectible in <i>Hollow Knight: Silksong</i>.

Use
Shell Shards are used to repair and craft the Tools that Hornet uses alongside her Needle. Tools can only be repaired when resting at a Bench and different Tools require a different number of Shell Shards to be repaired.

How to Acquire
Shell Shards can be gained in several different ways:
Defeating enemies
Destroying Shell Shard deposits
Breaking down Shard Bundles
